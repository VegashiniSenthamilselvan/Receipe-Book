Kaju Katli



Kaju Katli is a popular Indian sweet made from cashew nuts (kaju), sugar, and ghee. It's a beloved treat during festivals and special occasions, known for its rich, melt-in-the-mouth texture and delightful flavor. Here's a detailed description.

Kaju Katli is a delightful and rich sweet that is often enjoyed during Diwali, weddings, and other celebrations. Its simplicity and elegant taste make it a favorite among sweet lovers.

Ingredients

  • Cashews (Kaju): 1 cup
  • Sugar: 1/2 cup
  • Water: 1/4 cup
  • Cardamom Powder: 1/4 tsp
  • Ghee (Clarified Butter): 1 tsp

Instructions

  1. Grind the cashews to a fine powder using a dry grinder or food processor. Be careful not to grind too long, as it can turn into a paste.
  2. In a pan, mix sugar and water. Heat the mixture on medium flame until the sugar completely dissolves and forms a single-thread consistency syrup. This means the syrup should form a thin, unbroken thread when a small drop is pulled between two fingers.
  3. Add the cashew powder to the sugar syrup while continuously stirring to avoid lumps. Ensure the mixture is smooth.
  4. Continue to cook on low heat, stirring continuously. The mixture will start to thicken and leave the sides of the pan. This should take about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Add cardamom powder and ghee to the mixture. Mix well until the ghee is incorporated.
  6. Transfer the mixture to a greased plate or a piece of parchment paper. Allow it to cool slightly until it is safe to handle.
  7. Knead the mixture lightly to ensure it is smooth and free from lumps.
  8. Roll out the mixture using a greased rolling pin to about 1/4 inch thickness.
  9. Let it cool completely. Once cooled, cut the rolled-out mixture into diamond-shaped pieces using a sharp knife.
  10. Kaju Katli is ready to be served. It can be stored in an airtight container for up to a week.
