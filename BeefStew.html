Beef Stew



Beef Stew is a classic dish found in many cultures worldwide, each with its own variations. The basic premise involves simmering beef with vegetables in a flavorful broth until everything is tender and well-blended. Commonly used ingredients include root vegetables like carrots and potatoes, as well as aromatic herbs and spices.

Ingredients

  • Beef chuck or stew meat: 2 lbs, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • All-purpose flour: 1/4 cup
  • Olive oil: 2 tbsp
  • Onion: 1 large, chopped
  • Garlic: 3 cloves, minced
  • Carrots: 3, peeled and cut into chunks
  • Celery: 3 stalks, chopped
  • Potatoes: 3 large, peeled and cut into chunks
  • Tomato paste: 2 tbsp
  • Beef broth: 4 cups
  • Bay leaves: 2
  • Thyme: 1 tsp dried (or 1 tbsp fresh)
  • Rosemary: 1 tsp dried (or 1 tbsp fresh)
  • Worcestershire sauce: 1 tbsp
  • Frozen peas: 1 cup (optional)
  • Fresh parsley: chopped, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Season the beef cubes with salt and pepper. Dredge them in flour, shaking off any excess.
  2. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef cubes in batches, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan. Brown the beef on all sides, then remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add a bit more olive oil if needed. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  4. Add the carrots, celery, and potatoes to the pot. Stir to combine with the onions and garlic.
  5. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 2-3 minutes, allowing it to coat the vegetables and develop its flavor.
  6. Pour in the beef broth and red wine (if using), scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Return the browned beef to the pot.
  7. Add the bay leaves, thyme, rosemary, and Worcestershire sauce. Stir to combine. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for 2 to 2.5 hours, or until the beef and vegetables are tender.
  8. If using frozen peas, add them to the stew in the last 10 minutes of cooking.
  9. Remove the bay leaves. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.
